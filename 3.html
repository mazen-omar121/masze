Portable Electric Cement Mixer

In the construction industry's era of automation and power tools, the portable electric cement mixer stands out as a time-saving innovation.
By delegating the arduous task of mixing and pouring cement to a compact, electrically powered machine, workers can ensure consistent quality while slashing lead times.

Project Description

In the construction industry's age of automation and power tools, the advent of the portable electric cement mixer has significantly enhanced efficiency by delegating the laborious task of cement mixing and pouring to a portable, electrically powered machine. This innovation ensures consistent cement mix quality and reduces lead times. However, designing a reliable and safe machine capable of sustained use in rugged conditions is a complex endeavor. Understanding the mechanics behind an electric cement mixer reveals the necessity for a dependable power source, efficient power conversion mechanisms, and robust construction to withstand rough job site conditions. This case study delves into analyzing various parameters essential for ensuring the safety and functionality of such mixers, employing systematic methodology and engineering evidence for critical assessment. The problem statement focuses on analyzing a mixing drum tasked with blending a total mass of 150 kg of cement mixture, requiring specific inclinations and movements for optimal operation.

Design Requirements

The design requirements for the project encompass several key elements to ensure the functionality and efficiency of the system. Firstly, the drum must rotate within a specified range of 15 to 20 rpm in a clockwise direction, with dimensions not exceeding 1.0 m in diameter and depth. The system's power source is a 240V AC motor, requiring appropriate torque and power output for effective operation. Integration of gear reducers and belts with pulleys is essential to maintain the required rotational speed. Structural support is provided by frames, which can be either welded or bolted together. Meeting these specifications ensures the system's basic functionality. However, the project must also demonstrate logical and engineering soundness while being practically applicable. Therefore, the design process will employ methodologies used in modern power transmission systems to ensure a robust and effective solution.

Basis of Safe Design and Selection

Safety is paramount in the design of mechanical components for this project, encompassing bolts, motors, belts, and weld selection for both the system and its subsystems. The methodology outlined in later sections of the report is rooted in ensuring safe design practices. Beginning with the output stage, where the barrel of the mixer must rotate within a specified speed range, the selection process is reverse-engineered to prioritize safety. Starting with the required power and torque, derived from the rotational speed and mass of the cement, assumptions are made about the barrel's material and total mass. By calculating parameters such as mass moment of inertia, safety considerations are integrated into the design process from its inception, laying the foundation for a robust and secure system.
